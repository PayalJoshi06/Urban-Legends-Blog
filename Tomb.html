Overview

The Curse of King Tut's Tomb refers to the belief that those who enter or disturb the tomb of Tutankhamun, an ancient Egyptian pharaoh, will suffer misfortune or death.

It gained widespread attention after the discovery of Tutankhamun's tomb by archaeologist Howard Carter in 1922.

Discovery

In November 1922, Howard Carter, along with his sponsor Lord Carnarvon, discovered the tomb of Tutankhamun in the Valley of the Kings in Egypt.

The discovery of the relatively intact tomb, filled with treasures and artifacts, captured the world's imagination and sparked a renewed interest in ancient Egypt.

The Curse

Legend has it that a curse was placed upon the tomb to protect it from desecration. Those who entered the tomb or were involved in its excavation were said to suffer from illness, accidents, or even death.

Several individuals associated with the discovery of the tomb, including Lord Carnarvon, died under mysterious circumstances, fueling speculation about the curse's authenticity.

Legacy

Despite scientific skepticism, the Curse of King Tut's Tomb remains a popular topic of fascination and speculation.

It has inspired numerous books, films, and television shows, perpetuating the legend of the curse and contributing to the enduring mystique surrounding Tutankhamun and ancient Egypt.
