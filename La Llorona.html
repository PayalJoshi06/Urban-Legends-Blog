La Llorona

The Weeping Woman

La Llorona, or "The Weeping Woman," is a legendary figure in Latin American folklore, known for her tragic tale of love, betrayal, and eternal sorrow.

According to the legend, La Llorona was a beautiful woman named Maria who drowned her own children in a fit of jealousy and rage after her husband left her for another woman. Consumed by grief and remorse, she wanders the streets and rivers, crying out for her lost children.

Many claim to have seen her ghostly figure, dressed in white, wandering near bodies of water, wailing and searching for her children. It is said that encountering La Llorona is a harbinger of misfortune or even death.

The legend of La Llorona has been passed down through generations in Latin American culture, serving as a cautionary tale against jealousy, betrayal, and the consequences of one's actions.
