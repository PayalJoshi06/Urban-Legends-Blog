Overview

"The Legend of Sleepy Hollow" is a famous short story written by Washington Irving, first published in 1820 as part of his collection "The Sketch Book of Geoffrey Crayon, Gent."

The story is set in the secluded village of Sleepy Hollow, New York, and follows the mysterious events surrounding the Headless Horseman.

Characters

  • Ichabod Crane: The protagonist, a superstitious schoolmaster.
  • Brom Bones: A local resident and rival of Ichabod Crane.
  • Katrina Van Tassel: The beautiful daughter of a wealthy farmer, who both Ichabod and Brom compete for.
  • The Headless Horseman: A ghostly figure said to be a Hessian soldier who lost his head in battle and now haunts Sleepy Hollow.

Setting

Sleepy Hollow is depicted as a quiet, rustic village nestled in the Hudson Valley of New York. Its secluded and eerie atmosphere contributes to the story's haunting ambiance.

Plot

The story follows Ichabod Crane, a schoolmaster, as he becomes entangled in the legend of the Headless Horseman. Ichabod is vying for the affections of Katrina Van Tassel, but his romantic pursuits are thwarted by Brom Bones, who plays a prank on him involving the ghostly apparition.

One night, Ichabod encounters the Headless Horseman while riding home from a party at the Van Tassel farm. He disappears mysteriously, leaving only his hat behind. The townspeople speculate about his fate, with some believing he was taken by the Headless Horseman.
